Sources & method

🎙 Sound files — origin
Makasso, Emmanuel-Moselly & Seunghun J. Lee (2015). “Basaá.” Journal of the International Phonetic Association 45(1), 71–79 (Illustrations of the IPA). Supplementary audio © IPA / Cambridge University Press, obtained from Cambridge’s static CDN; research use.
Recorded from a male native Basaa speaker on a Marantz PMD661 with a Shure WH-30 head-worn mic. The 10 isolated items are the “syllable structure” utterances; the connected passage is Aesop’s North Wind & the Sun. Original WAVs transcoded to MP3 here.
Per-word time alignment: forced alignment with torchaudio’s MMS multilingual CTC aligner (child sentences word-level; NWS sentence-level reliable, word-level draft).
✍ Transcriptions — origin
Basaa orthographic/IPA forms are taken from the published illustration (Makasso & Lee 2015). The 10 child sentences are well recovered (tones provisional, to verify by ear). The North Wind & Sun word forms are provisional: the PDF’s IPA tier scattered on text extraction, so it should be re-transcribed against the audio (ideally with E.-M. Makasso).
🔖 Glossing — basis
Leipzig Glossing Rules, following Hamlaoui & Makasso’s own conventions — noun-class numbers (1-child, 6-doughnut), 1.AGR for the class-1 subject concord, 8.CONN for the connective, PRS/PST — grounded in Hamlaoui & Makasso (2014), “High tone spreading and Phonological Phrases in Bàsàá.” (Their full abbreviation key — FUT2, GEN, INF, LOC, OBL, PASS… — from the “Pronominal F-markers” paper is to be adopted.) Tense is glossed from meaning; its tonal exponent is left open.
🌍 Translation — basis
English: the free translations published in the IPA illustration (child sentences) and the standard English of Aesop’s passage. French: the conventional French of the passage (La bise et le soleil…) plus direct translations of the child sentences.
🌳 Syntax
Annotated in SUD (Surface-syntactic Universal Dependencies); rationale and open decisions in the project’s treebank/ notes. Subject concord is treated as bound verb morphology (1.AGR-…).
